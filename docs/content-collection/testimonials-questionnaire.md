# Content Collection — Testimonials

**Policy:** testimonials are **real only** — never written by us, never invented. The site shows a dignified "reserved" state until genuine words exist, then real quotes with no layout change. Goes into `content/testimonials.ts`.

---

## Part A — How to collect (workflow for the photographer)

1. **Ask after delivery**, when clients are happiest — include one line in your gallery-delivery email (template below).
2. **Get explicit permission** to publish their words + first name + city.
3. **Keep the source** (email/DM/Google) for your own records — it's stored in the data but never shown.
4. **Transcribe to text** — even if it came as a screenshot, we publish it as text (consistent, accessible, legible). Don't publish raw screenshots.
5. **Aim for 3–5 strong, specific quotes** to start; add more over time.

### Client request email (copy/paste, adapt)
> Bonjour [prénom],
> J'espère que vos photos vous plaisent ! Si vous avez aimé travailler ensemble, accepteriez-vous d'écrire **deux ou trois phrases** sur votre expérience ? Cela aide beaucoup les futures familles à me découvrir. M'autorisez-vous à publier vos mots avec votre **prénom** et votre **ville** ? Merci infiniment. — [nom]

---

## Part B — Questions to send the client (prompts for a good quote)

Ask the client to answer 1–3 of these in their own words:

1. **What were you unsure or nervous about before the session — and how did it go?**
2. **How did the session itself feel?**
3. **When you saw the photos, what did you think or feel?**
4. **What would you tell a friend who's considering booking?**
5. **One word for the experience?**

*(Short and specific is better than long and general. One honest sentence beats a paragraph of praise.)*
